History & Indigenous Culture

The Amazon Basin has been inhabited for at least 11,000 years. Ancient civilizations developed complex agricultural systems, including the famous Terra Preta, highly fertile man-made soil that still supports life today.

For the thousands of indigenous communities like the Yanomami and Ticuna, the river is not just water; it is a spiritual entity, a primary highway, and a source of ancestral knowledge. Their stewardship remains the primary defense against the encroaching threats of deforestation.

"The river is our umbilical cord to the earth." — Ticuna Elder
